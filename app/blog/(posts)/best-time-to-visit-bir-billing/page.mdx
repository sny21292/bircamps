# Best time to visit Bir Billing — a season-by-season guide

<img className="post-hero" src="/photos/Tent-Camping-in-Bir-Billing.webp" alt="Camping tents in Bir Billing with Dhauladhar mountains in the background" />

<p className="lead">Bir Billing is beautiful year-round, but the experience shifts with the seasons. Whether you're coming to fly, to camp, to trek or simply to breathe mountain air, here's what each month brings — and when to book.</p>

## The short answer

The **best months** for camping and paragliding in Bir Billing are **March to June** and **September to November**. Clear skies, mild weather and reliable flying conditions.

## Spring: March – May

Spring is arguably the best time to visit Bir Billing. The valley turns green, wildflowers appear along the trails, and the Dhauladhar peaks still carry snow.

- **Temperature:** 12°C – 28°C
- **Paragliding:** Excellent. Thermals build through March and the season hits its stride by April.
- **Camping:** Perfect. Warm days, cool evenings, bonfire weather every night.
- **Trekking:** Rajgundha valley opens up. Waterfalls start flowing at Gunehar and Bangoru.
- **Crowds:** Moderate. Book a week or two ahead for weekends.

> **Our pick:** Late March to mid-May is the sweet spot — warm enough for river walks, cool enough for bonfires, and the best flying weather of the year.

## Summer: June – mid-July

June is warm and lively. The pre-monsoon weeks bring dramatic clouds and golden light over the valley — great for photography. Paragliding is still on, though afternoon weather windows can shorten.

- **Temperature:** 18°C – 32°C
- **Paragliding:** Good in the mornings. Afternoon thermals can be too strong by late June.
- **Camping:** Warm, comfortable nights. Light rain possible towards July.
- **Good for:** Families, café culture, village walks, monastery visits.

## Monsoon: mid-July – mid-September

The rains bring Bir Billing to life — the valley is at its greenest and the waterfalls are at full force. But paragliding is suspended and trails can be slippery.

- **Temperature:** 16°C – 26°C
- **Paragliding:** Off-season. Flights resume in September.
- **Camping:** Possible but wet. We keep the camp running with covered areas and the café.
- **Best for:** Budget travellers, writers, anyone who likes rain on a tin roof.

## Autumn: September – November

The second peak season. The monsoon clears, the air is sharp and clean, and the Dhauladhars come back into view with fresh snow. This is when the Paragliding World Cup has historically been held in Bir Billing.

- **Temperature:** 10°C – 25°C
- **Paragliding:** Best conditions of the year. October is legendary for long flights.
- **Camping:** Cool nights, warm sleeping bags, stunning morning light.
- **Trekking:** Rajgundha is golden. Clear views all the way to the snow line.
- **Crowds:** Picks up around Dussehra and Diwali weekends. Book early.

> **For paragliding:** October is the single best month. If you can plan around it, do.

## Winter: December – February

Bir Billing gets cold but rarely freezing. Snowfall is uncommon in Bir itself (Billing and the higher ridges do get snow). The valley is quiet, the skies are blue, and the camp feels like your own private retreat.

- **Temperature:** 2°C – 15°C
- **Paragliding:** Limited. Some flights on clear days, but not guaranteed.
- **Camping:** Cold but beautiful. We provide extra blankets and heated water.
- **Best for:** Solitude, stargazing, reading by the fire, weekend getaways from Delhi.

## Month-by-month summary

| Month | Weather | Paragliding | Camping | Trekking |
|-------|---------|-------------|---------|----------|
| Jan–Feb | Cold, clear | Limited | Cold but quiet | Chilly |
| Mar–May | Warm, pleasant | Excellent | Perfect | Great |
| Jun | Hot, pre-monsoon | Good (AM) | Warm | Good |
| Jul–Aug | Rainy, green | Off | Wet | Slippery |
| Sep–Nov | Cool, clear | Best | Ideal | Best views |
| Dec | Cold, quiet | Limited | Cold | Chilly |

## What to pack by season

- **Spring/Autumn:** Layers, a light jacket, sunscreen, sunglasses, comfortable walking shoes.
- **Summer:** Light clothes, rain jacket, mosquito repellent.
- **Winter:** Warm jacket, thermals, wool socks, beanie. Nights drop to 2–5°C.
- **All seasons:** A power bank (charging is available but a backup helps), a torch for night walks, and a camera.

> Every season has its own character. Spring and autumn are the crowd favourites, but some of our best guests are the ones who come in December and have the whole valley to themselves.
