# How to reach Bir Billing from Delhi, Chandigarh & Manali

<img className="post-hero" src="/photos/camping-in-bir-billing.webp" alt="The road to Bir Billing through the Kangra valley, Himachal Pradesh" />

<p className="lead">Bir Billing sits in the Kangra valley of Himachal Pradesh, about 70 km from Dharamshala and roughly 530 km from Delhi. Getting here is part of the adventure — pine forests, valley switchbacks and mountain air the closer you get. Here's every route broken down.</p>

## From Delhi (530 km)

### By overnight bus (most popular)

The easiest option. Volvo and semi-sleeper buses run nightly from ISBT Kashmere Gate and Majnu ka Tilla to **Bir** or **Baijnath** (the nearest town).

- **Departure:** 6–8 PM from Delhi
- **Arrival:** 6–9 AM at Bir bus stop
- **Cost:** ₹800–₹1,500 depending on the operator
- **Operators:** HRTC (government), RedBus private operators

From Bir bus stop, Bir Camps is just 2 km away in Gunehr village — we can help arrange a pickup.

### By train + bus

Take a train from Delhi to **Pathankot** (7–9 hours), then a local bus or taxi to Bir (another 5–6 hours via Kangra and Baijnath). This route is scenic but longer overall.

### By flight + taxi

Fly to **Dharamshala (Gaggal Airport)** — the nearest airport, about 70 km from Bir. A taxi from Gaggal to Bir Camps takes around 2–2.5 hours and costs ₹1,500–₹2,000. Flights from Delhi take about 1.5 hours.

## From Chandigarh (260 km)

### By bus

Regular HRTC and private buses run from **ISBT Chandigarh** to Bir or Baijnath via Mandi.

- **Duration:** 7–9 hours
- **Cost:** ₹500–₹900
- **Route:** Chandigarh → Bilaspur → Mandi → Baijnath → Bir

### By taxi

A private taxi from Chandigarh to Bir takes about 6–7 hours and costs ₹3,500–₹5,000. This is the most comfortable option if you're in a group — split the fare and enjoy the mountain roads.

## From Manali (200 km)

Take a bus or taxi via the Kullu–Mandi highway and then through Baijnath to Bir. The road is winding but beautiful.

- **Duration:** 6–7 hours by bus, 5 hours by taxi
- **Bus cost:** ₹400–₹600
- **Taxi cost:** ₹3,000–₹4,000

## From Dharamshala / McLeod Ganj (70 km)

The shortest route. A local bus or taxi through the Kangra valley brings you to Bir in about 2–2.5 hours.

- **Bus cost:** ₹150–₹250
- **Taxi cost:** ₹1,500–₹2,000

## Nearest railway station

**Ahju** is the closest station on the Kangra Valley narrow-gauge line — a charming toy train, but very slow. The more practical station is **Pathankot**, well connected to Delhi, Mumbai and other cities. From Pathankot, take a bus or taxi to Bir (5–6 hours).

## Nearest airport

**Gaggal Airport (Dharamshala)** — about 70 km from Bir. Flights connect to Delhi and Chandigarh. From the airport, arrange a taxi or take a bus via Kangra.

## Tips for a smooth journey

- **Book overnight buses** from Delhi — you save a night's stay and arrive fresh in the morning.
- **Carry a light jacket** even in summer; the air gets cooler as you climb.
- **Download offline maps** — network coverage drops between Mandi and Bir.
