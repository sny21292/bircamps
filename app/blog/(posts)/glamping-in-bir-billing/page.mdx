# Glamping in Bir Billing — luxury camping in the Himalayas

<img className="post-hero" src="/photos/Swiss-Luxury-Camp.webp" alt="Swiss Luxury Camp at Bir Camps with Dhauladhar valley view, Bir Billing" />

<p className="lead">Glamping — glamorous camping — is the art of sleeping outdoors without giving up comfort. And Bir Billing, with its pine forests, river sounds and Dhauladhar backdrop, might be the best place in India to try it. Here's what glamping at Bir Camps looks like.</p>

## What makes it glamping?

Regular camping means a basic tent, a sleeping bag and roughing it. Glamping means you still wake up to mountain air and birdsong, but you sleep on a real bed with clean sheets, walk to a proper washroom, and have someone else cook your dinner. It's the outdoors — minus the discomfort.

At Bir Camps, three stays fit the glamping category:

## Swiss Luxury Camp — the signature experience

Our Swiss Luxury Camp is the most indulgent way to spend a night in Bir Billing. A spacious canvas tent set by the riverside with plush bedding, an ensuite setup and an uninterrupted view of the Dhauladhar valley.

**What's included:**
- Plush bedding and comfortable mattress
- Ensuite valley view
- Complimentary dinner, bonfire and breakfast
- Private bonfire for your group

This is luxury camping in Himachal Pradesh at its best — watch the light change over the mountains from the comfort of your tent, then step outside for a bonfire under the stars.

## Bell Camp — romantic and characterful

The Bell Camp is a classic canvas bell tent — tall, round, and full of character. It's the most popular choice for couples visiting Bir Billing and first-timers who want something special without the top-tier price.

**What's included:**
- Canvas bell tent with real bed
- Dinner, bonfire and breakfast
- Close to the riverside
- Wake to the sound of mountain water

Bell tents are iconic glamping — the shape alone makes you feel like you've stepped into another world. Ours are set close to the river, so you fall asleep to the sound of water.

## The Open Cottage — glamping with four walls

For those who want the glamping atmosphere but prefer solid walls, The Open Cottage is our newest stay — a wooden cabin that opens straight out onto the valley. A private deck, a proper bed, and the Dhauladhar range as your only neighbour.

This is glamping for people who love the outdoors but sleep better with a roof. Warm and snug in the evenings, flooded with light at dawn.

## Why Bir Billing for glamping?

Most glamping spots in India are either too remote, too expensive, or too commercialised. Bir Billing hits the sweet spot:

- **The setting:** A riverside camp in the Kangra valley, 70 km from Dharamshala, surrounded by pine forests and the Dhauladhar mountains.
- **The activities:** Tandem paragliding, the Rajgundha valley trek, waterfall walks, monastery visits, café hopping — all within easy reach.
- **The vibe:** Small, family-run, quiet. No loudspeakers, no party crowds. Just mountains, fire and good company.
- **The price:** Glamping at Bir Camps starts from ₹1,600 per person (Bell Camp) — a fraction of what you'd pay at resort-style glamping spots near Delhi or Manali.

## When to go glamping in Bir Billing

The best months are **March to May** and **September to November** — mild weather, clear skies, and perfect bonfire evenings. Winter glamping (December–February) is magical too: cold nights, warm blankets, empty valley, and stars you won't believe.
